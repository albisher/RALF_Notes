### SUMMARY
Describes common leaf arrangement patterns in plants, useful for botanical study and identification.

### DETAILS
This file categorizes the primary leaf arrangement types observed in plants, detailing how leaves are positioned relative to the stem. These patterns—**alternate, opposite, whorled, spiral, and random**—determine growth patterns, sunlight exposure, and structural adaptations. Alternate arrangements (e.g., *Aloe*) alternate along the stem, while opposite pairs (e.g., *Lavender*) face perpendicularly. Whorled arrangements (e.g., *Catalpa*) feature three or more leaves at a node, and spiral patterns (e.g., *Bamboo*) follow a helical trajectory. Random arrangements lack a fixed pattern, often seen in succulents. These classifications aid in plant taxonomy, ecology, and horticulture.

### KEY_FUNCTIONS
- **Alternate**: Leaves alternate along the stem (e.g., *Sunflower*).
- **Opposite**: Pairs of leaves emerge at the same node, opposite each other (e.g., *Ivy*).
- **Whorled**: Three or more leaves radiate from a single node (e.g., *Catalpa*).
- **Spiral**: Leaves grow in a continuous helical pattern (e.g., *Bamboo*).
- **Random**: No discernible pattern (e.g., *Cactus*).
