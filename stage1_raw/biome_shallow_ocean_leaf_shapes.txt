### SUMMARY
Describes leaf shape categories for shallow ocean biome representations, useful for biomechanical or ecological modeling.

### DETAILS
This file categorizes leaf-like shapes for shallow ocean biomes, specifically targeting frond-like, ribbon-like, membranous, and linear forms. These shapes are likely used in biomechanical simulations, ecological modeling, or artistic representations of aquatic flora. The classification reflects natural adaptations to shallow marine environments, where light availability and substrate stability influence growth patterns.

### KEY_FUNCTIONS
- **Frond-like (fern-like)**: Broad, leafy structures resembling ferns, common in shallow coral reefs or kelp forests.
- **Ribbon-like**: Long, flexible blades resembling seaweed (e.g., kelp), adapted for buoyancy and light capture.
- **Membranous (thin, film-like)**: Delicate, translucent structures (e.g., diatoms or some algae), optimized for nutrient absorption.
- **Linear (long and narrow)**: Elongated, streamlined forms (e.g., some macroalgae), reducing drag in water flow.
