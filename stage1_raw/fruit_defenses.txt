### SUMMARY
Explores natural fruit defenses against herbivores and predators through physical and chemical adaptations.

### DETAILS
This file outlines various mechanisms plants use to deter or harm predators, primarily through physical traits (e.g., thorns, spikes) and chemical compounds (e.g., toxins, irritants). These defenses evolve to balance protection against herbivory while ensuring seed dispersal. The mechanisms range from passive deterrents like acidic sprays to active responses like explosive seed dispersal, reflecting a trade-off between defense and reproduction.

### KEY_FUNCTIONS
- **Thorns/Spikes**: Physical barriers preventing consumption.
- **Hard Shell**: Protects internal tissues and seeds.
- **Toxic Flesh**: Non-lethal deterrents (e.g., bitter taste).
- **Lethal Toxin**: Directly kills predators (e.g., poisonous berries).
- **Explosive Seed Dispersal**: Startles predators to avoid seed loss.
- **Camouflage/Mimicry**: Deceptive traits to mislead predators.
