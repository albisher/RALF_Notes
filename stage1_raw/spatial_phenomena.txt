### SUMMARY
A compilation of key phenomena describing gravitational effects, spacetime dynamics, and relativistic/cosmological concepts.

### DETAILS
This file enumerates foundational and advanced spatial and temporal phenomena in theoretical physics, bridging classical mechanics with relativistic and quantum frameworks. It covers gravitational distortions (e.g., lensing, wormholes), extreme astrophysical objects (e.g., black holes), and cosmic-scale effects (e.g., dark energy, quantum fluctuations). The list serves as a reference for spacetime curvature, relativistic distortions (time/length), and emergent phenomena like Hawking radiation or Doppler shifts.

### KEY_FUNCTIONS
- **Gravitational Lensing**: Bending of light due to massive objects, acting as gravitational "magnifying glasses."
- **Space-time Curvature**: Metric distortion by mass/energy, governing spacetime geometry (Einstein’s theory).
- **Black Hole Ergosphere**: Region outside the event horizon where spacetime drags matter via frame-dragging.
- **Cosmic Expansion**: Universal scale-up driven by dark energy, altering redshift patterns.
- **Quantum Foam**: Hypothetical vacuum fluctuations in quantum gravity, influencing dimensional warping.

### DEPENDENCIES
