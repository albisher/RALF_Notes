### SUMMARY
Compilation of peer-reviewed research papers on multi-robot coordination, swarm robotics, and ROS2-based systems for HMRS project applications.

### DETAILS
This document serves as a curated bibliography for multi-robot coordination research, focusing on foundational algorithms, distributed control strategies, and practical implementations in robotic systems. It includes theoretical reviews (e.g., swarm engineering, graph-based formation control) alongside applied works (e.g., ROS2 architecture for heterogeneous teams). The content is tailored to the HMRS project, which employs three distinct drones (Scout, Tanker, Overseer) and emphasizes safety, autonomy, and inter-robot communication.

### KEY_FUNCTIONS
- **Swarm Robotics Review**: Identifies best practices for decentralized coordination in swarm-based systems.
- **Formation Control**: Provides graph-theoretic approaches for maintaining stable multi-robot formations.
- **Collision Avoidance Algorithms**: Offers distributed methods to prevent inter-robot collisions.
- **Heterogeneous Coordination**: Surveys strategies for integrating robots with varying capabilities.
