### SUMMARY
Describes plant type classifications for tundra biomes, emphasizing ice-adapted and surface-covering adaptations.

### DETAILS
This file categorizes plant types found in tundra ecosystems, focusing on adaptations suited to cold, icy environments. The classifications include **surface-covering organisms** (e.g., moss-analogues), **ground-cover plants** (e.g., grass-like species), **shrub-like forms**, and **ice-adapted flora** (cryo-flora). These adaptations reflect the tundra’s extreme conditions, such as permafrost, low temperatures, and limited nutrient availability.

### KEY_FUNCTIONS
- **Moss-analogue (surface covering)**: Low-growing, resilient ground cover that thrives in cold, nutrient-poor soils.
- **Grass-like Groundcover**: Short, hardy plants that tolerate freezing and compacted soil.
- **Shrub-like Form**: Dwarf shrubs with thick bark and deep roots for survival in harsh winters.
- **Cryo-flora (ice-adapted)**: Plants with specialized traits (e.g., antifreeze proteins, slow growth) to endure freezing temperatures and ice formation.

### DEPENDENCIES
none (standalone classification system)

### USAGE
This categorization is useful for ecological studies, climate modeling, or biodiversity assessments in polar or subarctic regions. Researchers may apply these classifications to analyze plant distribution, adaptation strategies, or ecosystem resilience.
