# biome_shallow_ocean_soil_types

## Summary

```
Describes soil type classifications for shallow ocean biomes, emphasizing organic and silicate-rich compositions.
```

## Details

> This file categorizes soil types found in shallow ocean environments, focusing on two primary compositions: **Organic-rich** (derived from decomposed marine organisms) and **Silicate-rich** (mineral-based, sourced from volcanic or sedimentary deposits). The term *Hydroponic* is likely a placeholder or misnomer here, potentially referencing nutrient-rich, water-based conditions rather than a distinct soil type.

## Key Functions

### `Organic-rich`

Represents soil enriched with decomposed organic matter (e.g., plankton, coral, or algae), critical for microbial activity and nutrient cycling in shallow marine ecosystems.

### `Silicate-rich`

Indicates mineral-dominated soil, often formed from weathered volcanic rock or sediment, providing essential minerals like calcium, magnesium, and phosphorus.

### `Hydroponic`

Describes a non-soil condition where nutrients are dissolved in water (e.g., nutrient films or recirculating aquaculture systems), though its relevance here may be context-dependent.

## Usage

This classification is typically used in:
- **Ecological modeling** of shallow marine biomes (e.g., coral reefs, mangroves).
- **Biofilm or sediment analysis** in oceanographic research.
- **Game/environment design** for biome-specific soil properties (e.g., game engines like Unity/Unreal).
