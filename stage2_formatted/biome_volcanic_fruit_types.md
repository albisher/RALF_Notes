## Details

> This document categorizes and defines distinct fruit types adapted to volcanic environments, where extreme conditions shape their physical traits. The listed types—**Mineralized Nodules**, **Capsules (dry, dehiscent)**, and **Geode-like Fruits (crystalline interior)**—reflect adaptations to nutrient-rich, unstable, or geologically active ecosystems. These traits likely aid survival by providing mineral reserves, protection, or dispersal mechanisms in harsh volcanic terrain.

## Key Functions

### `Mineralized Nodules`

Fruits containing concentrated minerals (e.g., silica, metals) for nutrient storage or defense.

### `Capsules (dry, dehiscent)`

Dry, split-open fruits that release seeds or spores in dry volcanic conditions.

### `Geode-like Fruits (crystalline interior)`

Fruits with a hard, crystalline core (e.g., quartz or volcanic glass) for protection or mineral accumulation.
