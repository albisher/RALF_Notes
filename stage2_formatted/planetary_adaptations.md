## Details

> This document outlines modular systems designed to support human or robotic operations on alien planets or moons. The technologies address critical challenges such as energy generation, propulsion, and protection against extreme conditions (e.g., radiation, temperature extremes, or gravity variations). Systems are categorized by function, including solar-powered energy solutions and magnetic propulsion for mobility, with additional layers like radiation shielding and atmospheric filtration to ensure habitability.

## Key Functions

### `Solar-powered systems`

Convert ambient light into energy for powering habitats or equipment.

### `Magnetic propulsion`

Uses magnetic fields to generate thrust, reducing fuel dependency in low-gravity environments.

### `Anti-gravity technology`

Stabilizes structures or vehicles in varying gravitational fields (e.g., Mars or exoplanets).

### `Water purification modules`

Filter and recycle water from atmospheric or planetary sources for drinking and life support.

### `Heat-resistant plating`

Protects surfaces from extreme thermal fluctuations (e.g., volcanic or desert-like environments).

### `Cold-resistant systems`

Maintain operational efficiency in sub-zero temperatures (e.g., polar regions or deep space).

### `Radiation shielding`

Absorbs cosmic or solar radiation to protect occupants from harmful radiation exposure.

### `High-gravity stabilizers`

### `Low-gravity thrusters`

### `Atmospheric filtration`

Purifies air for breathable conditions in non-Earth-like atmospheres.

## Usage

These systems are deployed in modular habitats, rovers, or orbital platforms. For example:
- **Habitats**: Solar panels + atmospheric filtration + radiation shielding for long-term survival.
- **Probes/Rovers**: Magnetic propulsion + low-gravity thrusters + heat-resistant components for exploration.
- **Research Stations**: High-gravity stabilizers + water purification + cold-resistant systems for extreme environments.
