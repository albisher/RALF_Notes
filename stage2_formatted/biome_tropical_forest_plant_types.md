## Details

> This document categorizes plant types found in tropical forests into four broad classifications: **Tree-like Form**, **Vine-like Climber**, **Epiphytic Alien**, and **Flowering Plant Analogue**. These categories help in understanding growth habits, ecological roles, and interactions within the biome. The classification is likely used for research, conservation, or ecological modeling to study biodiversity and resource distribution.

## Key Functions

### `Tree-like Form`

Represents woody plants with trunks and canopies, dominant in the forest structure.

### `Vine-like Climber`

Describes lianas and epiphytic vines that climb trees for support and access to sunlight.

### `Epiphytic Alien`

Identifies non-native plants growing on other plants (e.g., mosses, ferns, or bromeliads) that rely on host structures for stability.

### `Flowering Plant Analogue`

Compares flowering plants of tropical forests to terrestrial analogues, likely for evolutionary or functional studies.

## Usage

This classification can be applied in:
- **Field studies** to document plant diversity in tropical forests.
- **Conservation planning** to prioritize protection of dominant or invasive species.
