# plant_types

## Summary

```
Describes conceptual plant types for alien ecosystems, ecological analogs, and speculative biology.
```

## Details

> This file enumerates a diverse range of plant forms, adapted to alien environments, ecological niches, or fictional biology. Each entry represents a unique evolutionary or functional adaptation, useful for worldbuilding, game design, or scientific fiction. The list spans terrestrial, aquatic, and extreme-environment flora, including both organic and non-living analogs.

## Key Functions

### `Tree-like Form`

Dominant vertical growth for canopy structure.

### `Vine-like Climber`

Lianas or tendrils for support and nutrient absorption.

### `Grass-like Groundcover`

Low-growing, spreadable ground cover for biomass.

### `Carnivorous Plant`

Traps small organisms for nutrient acquisition.

### `Symbiotic Colony`

Hybridized plant-animal/fungi relationships.

### `Kelp-like Underwater Forest`

Aquatic photosynthetic structures.

### `Sentient Mobile Plant`

Slow-moving, possibly cognitive organisms.

### `Parasitic Plant`

Extracts nutrients from host organisms.

### `Bioluminescent Algae Mat`

Glowing surface coverings or colonies.

### `Crystalline Growth`

Plant-like mineral structures (e.g., non-biological).

### `Floating Gas Plant`

Buoyant, atmospheric nutrient-absorbing forms.

### `Lithophyte`

Rock-dwelling or consuming flora.

### `Thermo-flora`

Heat-adapted plants near volcanic vents.

### `Cryo-flora`

Ice-adapted organisms in cold environments.

### `Aerial Filter Feeder`

Absorbs atmospheric gases/nutrients.

### `Epiphytic Alien`

Parasitic growth on other plants.

### `Dominant Root Network`

Extensive below-ground or mixed systems.

### `Silicon-based Flora`

Non-carbon-based photosynthetic organisms.

### `Cactus-analogue`

Succulent-like storage adaptations.

### `Moss-analogue`

Surface-covering non-vascular ground cover.

### `Giant Fungi Colony`

Mycelial-based, often decomposer-like forms.

### `Floating Gas Plant`

Buoyant, atmospheric nutrient-absorbing forms.

### `Flowering Plant Analogue`

Reproductive structures in non-floral plants.

### `Crop-analogue`

Cultivable, resource-producing plants.
