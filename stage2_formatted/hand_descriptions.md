## Details

> This file outlines various hand descriptions for robotic or biomechanical applications, emphasizing different grip mechanisms and specialized functionalities. The entries cover a range of tactile and operational capabilities, from simple claw-like grippers to advanced multi-tool configurations. Each design is tailored to specific tasks, such as precision manipulation, material handling, or interaction with magnetic or suction-based surfaces.

## Key Functions

### `Three-fingered claw`

Provides a firm grip for heavy or rough materials.

### `Four-fingered hand`

Balances precision and stability for delicate or complex tasks.

### `Pincer grip`

Enables fine control and selective force application.

### `Magnetic pads`

Allows non-destructive attachment to ferrous materials.

### `Suction-based hand`

Ideal for lightweight, smooth surfaces (e.g., glass, plastic).

### `Multi-tool hand`

Combines multiple functions (e.g., gripping + cutting) in one unit.

### `Drill-equipped hand`

Specialized for drilling or marking materials.

### `Laser-cutting hand`

Uses laser precision for cutting or engraving.

### `Extendable fingers`

Increases reach or adaptability for tight spaces.

### `Soft-touch rubberized hand`

Reduces damage to delicate surfaces with gentle interaction.
