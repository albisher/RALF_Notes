# biome_tundra_climate_types

## Summary

```
Describes climate characteristics of the **subglacial** and **nocturnal** conditions in tundra biomes.
```

## Details

> This document outlines the specific climate conditions associated with **subglacial** (under ice) and **nocturnal** (nighttime) environments within tundra ecosystems. Subglacial regions experience extreme cold and pressure due to ice cover, while nocturnal conditions highlight temperature fluctuations and seasonal darkness typical of polar tundras. These factors influence microbial life, permafrost stability, and carbon cycling in Arctic and sub-Arctic environments.

## Key Functions

### `Subglacial Climate`

Describes thermal and pressure dynamics beneath glacial ice.

### `Nocturnal Tundra`

Explains temperature drops, polar night effects, and energy balance during darkness.

## Usage

This document serves as a reference for researchers studying tundra ecology, climate modeling, or glacial environments. It can be used to contextualize field observations or simulations of subglacial/nocturnal processes.
